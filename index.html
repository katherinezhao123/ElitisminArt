Museum of Modern Art

Founded in 1929, MoMA has been a source of aspiration and inspiration for budding artists. It is one of the most notable and largest collection of modern art pieces in the world.

Metropolitan Museum

Located on Fifth Avenue, the largest art museum in the country is known for housing rare, ancient pieces. Every year, the annual Met Gala is hosted there, drawing celebrities and high society to show off their finest costumes.

Brooklyn Museum

Home to 1.5 million art pieces in the borough of Brooklyn, this museum was founded in 1823, with original plans of becoming the largest art muesum in the world. It is home to the Memorial Sculputure Garden, with elements from all five boroughs.

How can we stop the exclusion of beginning artists?

While eliminating eltism in a field seems to be a task too big for anyone to take, there are steps that consumers and enjoyers of the art world can easily implement.

Spreading Awareness

The simple act of informing others about the problems within the art society can lead to a change.

Curating to the Public

Curators of these top showcases and galleries are the ones who select which pieces can be viewed and displayed before millions. They have the power to change the status quo.

Support More Independent Creators

Attend more piblic art showings, fairs, and galleries. Money talks, and the diversion from elite institutions will grab attention.
